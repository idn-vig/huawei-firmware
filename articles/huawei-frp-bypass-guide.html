Factory Reset Protection (FRP) is a security feature introduced in Android 5.1 that requires you to verify the previously synced Google or Huawei account after a factory reset.

When Does FRP Activate?

FRP lock appears when:

  • Factory reset is performed via recovery mode
  • Device is reset through Find My Device
  • Multiple failed unlock attempts trigger reset
  • Device is flashed with new firmware

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Huawei FRP?
FRP (Factory Reset Protection) is a security feature that locks your Huawei device after factory reset, requiring the original Google or Huawei account credentials to access the device.
Can FRP be bypassed without losing data?
In most cases, the device already went through factory reset before FRP appears, so data is already gone. Some methods can preserve data if device wasn't fully reset.
Is FRP bypass legal?
Bypassing FRP on your own device is legal. It becomes problematic only when done on stolen devices. We require proof of ownership for our services.
Will FRP come back after bypass?
No, once properly bypassed, FRP won't return unless you add a new Google/Huawei account and then factory reset again.
How long does FRP bypass take?
Most FRP bypasses are completed within 30 minutes to 2 hours, depending on device model and lock type.

Prevention Tips

To avoid FRP issues in the future:

  • Remember your accounts - Keep credentials in password manager
  • Remove account before reset - Sign out of Google/Huawei before factory reset
  • Use "Erase all data" - From Settings instead of Recovery mode
  • Keep proof of purchase - In case you need official support
